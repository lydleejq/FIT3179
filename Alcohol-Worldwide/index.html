Alcohol Consumption and its Impacts

Alcohol consumption across the globe

This interactive map shows the annual average alcohol consumption of alcohol, expressed per person aged 15 years or older. The global average consumption was 6.18 liters per person in the latest year available. The average amount of alcohol consumed per person around the world varies greatly, as the map illustrates. We see notable geographic variations: alcohol use in North Africa and the Middle East is almost nonexistent.

Select a year from the dropdown menu to explore global alcohol consumption of that year. Hover over each country to learn more.

What is each country's favourite drink?

The radial plots depict the types of alcoholic beverages (spirit, beer, wine) consumed by select countries in 2014, and are placed side by side to aid with comparison. Australia and Japan have been selected by default. The most popular type of alcoholic beverage in Australia is beer, while for Japan spirits is the clear winner.

Select two countries from the dropdown menus to compare their alcohol consumption.

Analysis of premature deaths caused by alcohol

The world's economies are divided into four income groups by the World Bank: low, lower-middle, upper-middle, and high income. We will investigate how premature death rates caused by alcohol correlates with this classification.

Premature death rates due to alcohol

The line chart shows the age standardized rate of deaths per 100,000 people. Globally the rate has declined from 43 deaths per 100,000 people in the early 1990s to 35 deaths in 2017.

Among the four income groups, low income countries have the highest number of deaths due to alcohol.

Alcohol related deaths by income and age

The area chart shows the age distribution of those dying premature deaths due to alcohol. Globally almost three quarters are younger than 70 years. 28 percent are younger than 50 years.


Select a World Bank country classification from the dropdown menu to explore how they affect the proportion of age groups.

Prevalence of alcohol use disorders, males versus females

Alcohol use disorder refers to drinking of alcohol that causes mental and physical health problems. Globally, 108 million people are estimated to have an alcohol use disorder. 77 percent globally (83.5 million) were male relative to 24.5 million females in 2019.
