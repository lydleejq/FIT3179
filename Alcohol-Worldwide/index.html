Alcohol Consumption and its Impacts

Alcohol Consumption Across the Globe

This interactive map shows the annual average alcohol consumption of alcohol, expressed per person aged 15 years or older. To account for the differences in alcohol content of different alcoholic drinks (e.g. beer, wine, spirits), this is reported in liters of pure alcohol per year.
Select a year to see how alcohol consumption was for that year.

Type of Alcohol Consumed by Each Country

Use the dropdown menu to explore how the alcohol type consumed differs across the globe.

Alcohol is responsible for 2.8 million premature deaths each year.

Drag the slider to learn how alcohol use ranks among other risk factors over the years.

Alcohol related deaths by age

The chart shows the age distribution of those dying premature deaths due to alcohol. Globally almost three quarters are younger than 70 years. 28% are younger than 50 years.

Rates of premature deaths due to alcohol across regions

The chart shows the age standardized rate of deaths per 100,000 people. Globally the rate has declined from 43 deaths per 100,000 people in the early 1990s to 35 deaths in 2017. Among the four country groups, low income countries have the highest number of deaths due to alcohol.
